Ancient Graffiti Project 2013.5

The Ancient Graffiti Project is a website that provides a search engine for locating and studying graffiti of the early Roman empire from the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum. I am one of the four developers that have started implementing the infrastructure, in May 2013. Our search engine allows three different types of searches.

  • You can search for graffiti by location, selecting either the pull-down menu, or by clicking on the map, or
  • You can search specifically for graffiti drawings by choosing the class of drawing that interests you, or
  • You can search for a specific word or phrase and find where it occurs within the ancient city.

I have shown how to use the "map search" in the video clip, and you're welcome to explore the rest of the project and functionalities yourself. Currently, it has a lot of new features than when I am working on it.

In this project, my main responsibility is to carve out the design of the website, so I dealt much with HTML, CSS and box models.
